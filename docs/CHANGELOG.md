## v0.18 · 2026-07-12 "Hold Your Ground"
A follow-up on v0.17: Rodolfo asked for the boss fight to have more mechanics (more dialogue, real difficulty, its own music, something that actually breaks the week's monotony instead of feeling like just another card), plus a separate cleanup request.
- **The Friday Meeting is now a real escalating exchange, not one binary choice.** Choosing to push back at the opening no longer just ends in a flavor line, it leads into a second beat: the boss doesn't let it go either, and you get one more real decision — hold your ground (a genuine gamble) or let it drop (safe, no further cost). Staying quiet at the opening skips this entirely; there's nothing to escalate if you never pushed back.
- **Holding your ground is a real risk, with real stakes.** Win, and the meeting claims one fewer item this Friday (on top of whatever the boss rotation already determined), plus a small Reputation/Sanity recovery. Lose, and it costs meaningfully more Reputation and Sanity than staying quiet ever would have. Odds are weighted by your current Reputation (a stat you've been building all week, now has a use at the moment that matters most), get worse at higher weeks, and are deliberately worse against Miller specifically than any of the three directors, keeping him hardest on yet another axis.
- **New dialogue**: 3 opening rebuttal lines, 3 win lines, 3 loss lines, all boss-name-aware so the exchange still reads as being with whichever of the four is running that day.
- **The whole meeting now sounds and looks different from the rest of the week.** A new `inBossFight` flag pushes the existing Sanity/week-driven dread music noticeably further for the entire meeting (not just a one-off sting), and a new pulsing red vignette (`body.confrontation`) wraps the screen for the same span, both switched on the moment 4 PM starts and guaranteed to clear afterward no matter which ending is reached (survived, malicious compliance, quit, Burnout, or Performance Managed Out).
- **Removed the ID badge boot graphic** (the small circular silhouette added in v0.12/v0.13), along with its CSS, HTML, and the `.glitchy` escalation logic tied to it. The badge-photo-doubling motif stays in the narrative text (Burnout ending, Two Weeks' Notice ending) since only the visual asset itself was cut, not the idea.
- Verified via `node --check`, a clean dominance-audit re-run, and a headless jsdom simulation forcing both the win and lose paths through the full sequence: confirmed correct claims reduction on a win, correct Reputation/Sanity deltas on both outcomes, and confirmed the confrontation visual/audio state is present throughout the meeting and fully cleared after every ending path.

## v0.17 · 2026-07-12 "Before He Starts Talking"
A scope correction: what Rodolfo actually asked for last session was a boss *battle*, an interactive confrontation with real stakes, not just a rotating cast running the same passive reveal-and-claim sequence. That's a real gap, not a misunderstanding on his end.
- **The Friday Meeting is no longer purely passive.** Right as the room fills in, before any names get read, you now get one real choice: meet the boss's eyes and push back out loud (costs Reputation, zero Sanity hit), or say nothing and let it happen (costs Sanity, no Reputation change). This is the first point in the entire meeting where the player does something instead of watching it happen to them.
- **Sized deliberately small.** Presented three options (a per-claim defense choice, this confrontation beat, and a full turn-based composure-meter combat system) and Rodolfo picked the middle one: reuses the exact card/choice UI every other encounter in the game already uses, touches only Sanity/Reputation (not claims, targeting, or shield threshold, which stayed untouched to protect last session's tuning), and ships as one clean binary choice rather than a new sub-system. The composure-meter option was flagged as likely scope creep for a first pass.
- **Side benefit: this directly helps the standing "hard to lose under good play" problem** (flagged since v0.3-era notes, still open as of v0.13-v0.16). The Friday Meeting previously cost almost no Sanity on its own; now every single week guarantees a real Sanity choice regardless of how well you prepared, adding a genuine weekly pressure valve toward Burnout that pure preparation can't fully route around.
- Verified via `node --check`, the dominance audit (clean, not flagged since delta profiles diverge on opposite axes with no strict winner), and a headless jsdom run driving both choices through to the existing reveal/verdict, confirming correct Sanity/Reputation application and a clean handoff into the rest of the meeting.

## v0.15 · 2026-07-12 "Whoever Shows Up"
Requested feature: the Friday Meeting is no longer always Miller.

- **Miller is now AVP Miller**, sitting above a roster of three directors (Reed, Brooks, and a new third: Calloway). The Handbook's Friday-mechanic explanation was rewritten to describe a rotation instead of one fixed person; Per Director Reed's flavor text was adjusted so its "other two directors" line still tracks now that there are exactly two others besides Miller.
- **Any of the four can run 4 PM, revealed only when the slide goes up.** Weeks 1-2 are always Miller, so the player learns the baseline before the roster starts rotating; week 3 onward it's a weighted roll (Miller 55%, Reed/Brooks/Calloway 15% each), chosen once at the start of each week (`buildWeek()`) and kept secret until `meetingProper()` runs. When a substitute is covering, a short absence line explains where Miller is (aligning with Upstairs, letting Reed cover; "volunteering" Brooks; Calloway covering "with love").
- **Miller is mechanically the hardest on every axis, not just flavor.** Substitutes claim one fewer item than Miller would at every difficulty tier (guaranteed by formula, not tuned by hand), and Miller's Reputation shield threshold (70) is the highest of the four, meaning he's the hardest to protect your best item against even though he's otherwise the "default" boss. Reed (65), Brooks (55), and Calloway (45) are each easier to shield against, in that order.
- **Each director plays differently, not just reads differently:**
  - **Director Reed** targets whichever item is already NOTICED, not the highest-value one, and is immune to the Decoy Deliverable trinket entirely, she doesn't work off the same tell everyone else does.
  - **Director Brooks** uses the same value-based targeting and decoy interaction as Miller, just claims less.
  - **Director Calloway** has a hidden Sanity tax: mid-meeting she asks the room to "share a rose and a thorn," costing Sanity that isn't shown anywhere on the At Risk screen going in, on theme for a toxic-positivity character whose real cost only shows up after you've already agreed.
- **New card: "A Note From Director Calloway"** (unlocks week 2), introducing her ahead of her first possible Friday appearance: a DM asking you to post a "win" in `#gratitude`, with genuinely different trade-offs (performing gratitude publicly for Reputation vs. a private honest reply that costs Reputation but saves Life Outside), audited clean against the dominated-choice checker.
- **Credit-flow text branches by who's running the room.** Miller's version is unchanged ("presents it Upstairs to The President"); a substitute's version notes the credit converts to their Raise Points "on paper," but still structurally rolls up to Miller on the org chart, keeping the satire's actual target (the structure, not whichever individual is standing at the podium) intact. The bigger end-of-run summary line (`scoreBlock()`, "Claimed by AVP Miller") and the Two Weeks' Notice / Malicious Compliance endings were deliberately left anchored to Miller specifically, on the reasoning that those are epilogue beats about beating the institution as a whole, not about whichever director happened to run that particular Friday.
- Verified via `node --check`, a re-run of the dominance audit (clean, including the new Calloway card), and a headless jsdom simulation forcing each of the four bosses with a fixed item set: confirmed correct claims counts per boss/week tier, Reed's noticed-first targeting and decoy immunity, Brooks' decoy compatibility, Calloway's hidden Sanity tax applying through the real cost pipeline (including the existing settling-surcharge scaling), asymmetric shield thresholds (Calloway shielded at Rep 50, Miller was not), and a ~55/15/15/15 boss distribution over 4,000 simulated week-5 rolls with weeks 1-2 always Miller across 20 trials each.

## v0.13 · 2026-07-11 "The Cost of Leaving"
Two more live-playtest reports: a visual bug and a difficulty signal (beaten 3+ times, zero losses).
- **Bugfix: badge-photo silhouette bled outside its circle.** The shoulders path in the new boot visual extended past the outer ring at the bottom corners. Added an SVG `clipPath` matching the ring so the silhouette is always cropped to the circle regardless of exact path coordinates, instead of relying on hand-tuned numbers staying inside the boundary.
- **Bugfix: THE STOIC (and any contract) always read "not met" on the Two Weeks' Notice ending**, even when the condition was clearly satisfied (reported: ended with 97 Sanity, contract required 60+, still showed not met). Root cause: the quit ending returns immediately and never calls `awardLeverage()`, which is the only place `S.contractMet` was ever set. Fixed by evaluating `S.contract.check()` directly in the quit branch. Since that ending wipes the save immediately (new game, not continue), the display now shows "MET" without an inaccurate "+4 Leverage" promise that would never actually be banked.
- **Escape Plan costs raised 25/25/30 → 40/40/45 (80 → 125 total).** Signal: escaped 3+ times, never lost once. The Leverage economy let a strong week (Grade A, contract met) reach escape in ~5-6 weeks, meaning most players never saw the week-8/9 difficulty tiers (the harshest ones) before winning. At the new total, a near-perfect week (~18 Leverage) needs ~7 weeks minimum; a solid-but-not-perfect week (~8 Leverage) needs ~16. This was the "slow the escape" option, chosen over sharpening the early game or hand-tuning loss conditions, specifically because it's a single legible number change (visible directly in the store) rather than new hidden systems, and it structurally forces exposure to the ramp that already exists instead of building a new one. Flagged for Rodolfo to re-test: if this makes the mid-game feel like a slog rather than tense, the next lever is the Leverage income formula itself, not another cost bump.

## v0.12 · 2026-07-11 "The Decoy"
Two bugfixes from live playtest feedback, plus Phase 3 of the design-review implementation plan.
- **Bugfix: evening events could repeat two nights running.** `eveningEvent()` picked from the EVENINGS pool with plain `Math.random()` and no memory of what ran last, so the same beat (reported: "Old Friend, Late Call" twice in a row) could land back to back. Added `S.lastEvening` tracking and a `pickEvening()` helper that excludes last night's pick whenever another option exists. Verified with 20,000 simulated draws: zero consecutive repeats.
- **Bugfix: Old Friend's choice had no real trade-off.** Both options healed Sanity with no downside, and the "worse" option (+5) was strictly dominated by the better one (+12), so there was never a reason to pick it. Rebalanced against the existing Life Outside system instead of a flat number: going out heals less (+8) but feeds the friend meter (`life: 1`); the rain check heals more (+12, now the numerically "optimal" choice) but costs it (`life: -1`). The efficient play is now quietly the isolating one, on theme for a game about grind culture, and the friend-meter cost gives the "worse" option a real reason to exist.
- **A Decoy Deliverable (new store trinket, 18 Leverage).** The first item in the store that changes *how* a system plays out instead of nudging a number, per the design review's Phase 3 ask. Thursday night, if owned, name one unlocked item as the one Director Miller finds first; he claims it ahead of the natural value-sort at Friday's meeting, doesn't change how many items he takes, changes which one. Lets a cheap item absorb a claim that would otherwise have landed on your best one. Verified in a headless run: a decoy set on a 4-value item protected a 14-value item ("The Dashboard") that would otherwise have been the natural top target.
- **Unreliable narrator, below 30 Sanity.** Building on the existing narrator-inversion text drift, the terminal now occasionally (1 card in 5, while dissociating) misstates what a choice costs: the button's label is untouched, but the actual Sanity hit on click runs 2-5 higher than advertised, revealed only in the result text ("That cost more than it said it would."). Never applied to choices that skip the result screen, so the discrepancy always has somewhere to land. Verified via simulation at the expected trigger rate.
- **Badge-photo boot visual.** A small circular inline-SVG silhouette (no likeness, hand-coded) now appears at boot, captioned "EMPLOYEE ID · PHOTO ON FILE," tying into the existing "your badge photo has been retaken, you were not present for this" boot line. From week 5 on, or once 2+ Escape Plan items are owned, it quietly doubles on itself in a slow flicker, a literal "photo on file" that doesn't quite match what's actually there anymore.

## v0.10 · 2026-07-11 "Your Call"
Phase 1 of the 2026-07-11 design review's implementation plan (see `Friday_Meeting_Design_Review_2026-07-11.md`): the roguelike-agency fix plus two presentation upgrades.
- **Tuesday and Thursday are now a real choice, not a fixed sequence.** Those two days' three free slots are dealt face-up (title + tag only, no flavor or choices spoiled) as a "YOUR CALL" screen; pick one to resolve it now, and the pool shrinks to two, then the last card plays automatically once it's the only one left. - **Real terminal font:** swapped system Courier for [Share Tech Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Share%2BTech%2BMono) (Google Fonts, free, OFL-licensed), loaded via a single `<link>`, with Courier New as fallback if the font fails to load. Chosen over VT323 for legibility at the small sizes (0.7–0.9em) most of the UI runs at.
- **Sanity now visibly sickens the screen, continuously**, not just at the one-shot Burnout glitch: `#app`'s CSS filter now scales saturation, contrast, and a slow hue-rotate with `(100 - Sanity)/100`, recalculated every render and eased with a 1.2s transition. At full Sanity the screen looks exactly as before; near zero it's desaturated, higher-contrast, and faintly off-hue.
