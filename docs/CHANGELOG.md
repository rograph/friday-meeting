# Changelog

## v0.8 · 2026-07-11 "The Building Remembers"
- **Company renamed:** Corestone Dynamics is now **Everwell Group**, tying the company's own name into the wellness satire already in the game (the Mandatory Wellness Module, the Wellness Check-In evening event).
- **Small choices now echo on Friday instead of just resolving in the moment.** Two or more declined evening voluntolds raise the Reputation bar for the Friday shield from 70 to 80 ("Busy weekend?" delivered live at 4 PM, -4 Rep); two or more accepted with zero declines lowers it to 65 and adds a "Reliable" beat instead. The Rumor now pays off Friday too (squash it: +2 Rep at the Meeting; leave it: -3 Rep). An HR inquiry now gets your top item Noticed and earns a bitter aside from Miller on Friday. An unauthorized Facilities fix now arrives Noticed. Hanging vs. slow-walking Walsh's portrait sets a same-day mood flag that shaves or adds 1 Time to that day's lock cost.
- **The boss's claim line now varies:** six variants for "he takes credit for your work" at the Friday Meeting, picked with no immediate repeat, so the theft beat doesn't read as copy-pasted week over week.
- **The File Cabinet:** new top-bar button and modal with two sections, Completed Contracts (every week's optional objective, met or not) and Saved Files (the "+Leverage, the ledger grows" beats from The Offboarding, The Line Item, The Matcha Machine, Farewell Cake, The Austerity Memo, and the new Backfill card, now a real visible list). Also added a free "file one item away" pick in the Saturday store, once a week.
- **Eight new cards** (The Kudos Channel, Password Reset Again, The Post-Meeting Meeting, The Printer Ate Something Important, Onboarding Buddy: Jordan, The All-Staff Survey, The Shared Calendar, The Backfill That Never Comes), five unlocked at week 1. Week 1's pool was only 12 cards against 10 draws (83% of the deck shown every week); it's now 17 (about 59%). Deck is 40 cards by week 7.
- **The boot screen is quieter:** the growing wall of "POLICY UPDATE" / "NOTICE" lines is now collapsed into a single "▸ POLICY NOTICES (N) · CLICK TO REVIEW" line, opened on demand in a modal, instead of typed out in full every Monday.
- **The chairs at Daily Standup now change, subtly, every week:** one more chair than last week, tighter spacing, a chair facing the door, one short, a warm seat nobody sat in, a name in pencil that isn't anyone's, exactly as many chairs as employees, a circle that's quietly not a circle anymore, and more. Twelve variants, never explained.

## v0.7 · 2026-07-11 "Fridays Are Different Here"
- **Difficulty up a notch:** evening recovery reduced across the board (the trains run shorter now); the settling surcharge starts week 3 (+1 Sanity on everything) and deepens week 6 (+2); from week 7 Miller claims THREE items ("HE IS BEING CONSIDERED FOR SOMETHING"); and from week 3 the tracker notices your best exposed item by itself every Wednesday, no human involved.
- **Every week arrives differently:** 8 rotating boot greetings ("YOUR CHAIR HAS BEEN ADJUSTED. NOT BY YOU.") and 8 rotating building-status lines ("CONFERENCE ROOM B REMAINS OUT OF SERVICE. THE TABLE IS BEING STUDIED."). The goal is also on the boot screen now: once you own Escape items, the terminal notes your outside activity ("THIS IS NOT A THREAT. IT IS A COUNT."), and at 3/3: "THE LETTER IN YOUR BAG HAS BEEN NOTED. IT HAS ALWAYS BEEN NOTED."
- **Boot theme:** first click on the title screen wakes a slow music-box figure over the drone, each note trailed by a detuned ghost of itself. The melody ends when you clock in; the drone, like the job, continues.
- **Fridays are special:** the FRIDAY title card turns red and arrives on a rising "flashback" swell (noise through a sweeping filter, then a hard cut), and the 4 PM meeting opens with the same riser plus a half-second white flash, like memory.
- **Three lore cards (fictionalized):** *The Priority* (the hallway poster must become a framed photo of VP Walsh, ASAP, expedited "like an organ for transplant"), *The Handoff* (the IT intern, the remote, the broken conference table, the exit at a speed just under running), *The Bookshelf* (pristine spines, approximate quotes, "you cannot tell if he reads the books; you cannot tell when he is lying; these may be the same skill").
- Deck is now 32 cards by week 7.

## v0.6 · 2026-07-11 "The Whole Backlog"
All twelve researched backlog items, plus five new lore cards.
- **Death recap:** Burnout now itemizes exactly where your Sanity went, by source. Losses are legible.
- **Weekly contracts:** one optional objective each Monday (Clean Week, Archivist, Stoic, Diplomat, Ghost), +4 Leverage if met, shown under the stats bar and scored at week's end.
- **Card unlock schedule:** new saves start with 12 personnel files; the rest unlock weekly through week 7 ("PERSONNEL UPDATE: 3 NEW FILES ADDED TO YOUR ROTATION").
- **Weekly modifiers:** one policy drawn per week from week 2 (Audit Week: locks cost +1 but each locked item pays +1 Leverage; Quota Week: 15 Credit by Wednesday EOD or -8 Rep; Strategic Partner: the vendor starts active; or no update, "THIS IS NOT REASSURING").
- **The Expense Report:** an interactive Friday form that gains one field per week, forever, from DATE through BLOOD TYPE. Click each field; the autofill values are pre-approved.
- **Life Outside:** the Saturday store now shows the plant, the friend, and the hobby. Free evenings feed them; voluntolds starve them.
- **Narrator inversion:** below 30 Sanity, card text drifts, words repeat repeat, and the terminal begins describing your choices before you make them ("You have already decided. The terminal is simply waiting.").
- **Animations:** claimed items physically slide off your side of the table at the Meeting; paper trails slam a FILED ink stamp; the screen dims as Time runs out; Malicious Compliance releases exactly one balloon.
- **The Elevator (week 6+, once per save):** the doors are already open. Someone in a suit the color of the building nods at you, precisely once. The keyhole floor is not in the directory.
- **Five lore cards (fictionalized):** *The Farewell Cake* (turnover has tripled in three years, a statistic that lives in the frequency of cake), *Coffee with Nina* (a real ally; earn her trust once and The Network costs 10 less, she made a call), *Per Director Reed* (nobody under her likes her; Miller implements everything she thinks), *The Procurement Paradox* (eleven expedited plastic plants, month eleven of CRM review), *The Austerity Memo* (four-cent cups banned; two engraved parking signs installed the same afternoon).
- Store layout bug fixed: owned items no longer overflow the frame.
- Deck is now 29 cards + the elevator. Cast additions: Nina, Director Reed, Doug's colleagues in spirit.

## v0.5 · 2026-07-10 "The Building Sounds Wrong"
- **Reactive music:** the ambient drone now tracks your state. As Sanity falls, the detuned twin beats faster (a slow shimmer becomes a nauseous wobble) and the minor second swells from a whisper to a presence. Week number raises the floor of the dread, so later weeks never sound fully calm. Losing players hear it before they see it.
- **CRT day-wipe:** each day ends by collapsing to a bright horizontal line, then the next morning powers on. A soft two-note chime marks the new day.
- **New SFX:** store purchases, vendor inbox pings, day chime, and a true typewriter on the boot screen: sharp metallic strike plus dull body thock per character, a faint carriage-return bell at each line end, slow deliberate pacing with occasional hesitations, as if the terminal is deciding what to tell you. Click anywhere to skip.
- **Miller leaves early (new mechanic):** 40% of evenings, his office is dark by 3:04 PM ("he left for a thing, he always has a thing") and paper trails cost 1 less that night. His absences are your openings.
- **New card, The Retrospective:** Miller trash-talks Director Reyes, retired three years, whose systems still run the department unattended. Nod along, or mention that mildly and end the meeting four minutes early.
- **New card, The Matcha Machine:** VP Walsh's kitchen-remodel non-negotiable, polished weekly, untouched for 14 months. Make the first matcha in over a year (+6 Sanity, "two wrongs, one perfect green tea") or add it to your quiet ledger of institutional waste (+1 Leverage, "the ledger is patient").
- Deck is now 24 cards, 10 dealt per run.

At 70 or above, your best unlocked item is shielded at the Friday Meeting: his eyes slide off it like it's laminated. The shield never grants a free Malicious Compliance.

- **Help screen:** a "? HANDBOOK" button opens the Employee Handbook (Abridged). "This handbook is provided for your convenience. It is not a contract. Nothing is a contract."
- **Two new cards (fictionalized):** *The Head Start*, Director Brooks assigns you the task you started an hour ago ("Amazing turnaround!" The turnaround was negative one hour.). *The Escalation Path*, VP Walsh's chat status is green, he is typing, and the true business of his day is soccer; make the decision yourself and he hearts it eleven minutes after kickoff ends.
- Deck is now 21 cards, 10 dealt per run.

- **Engine pivot (Decision 9):** v1 is now vanilla HTML/JS. The game is `index.html` at repo root, GitHub Pages serves it with no build step. Godot skeleton shelved (safe to delete locally); the Godot-export GitHub Actions workflow is obsolete.
- **First fully playable run:** Monday–Friday, 10-card shuffled deck + fixed Standup (Mon) and 1:1 with the boss (Wed), evening events (free time / voluntold), At Risk tracker with paper-trail locks and Thursday-midnight lock-in, the Friday 4 PM Meeting boss encounter, and three endings: Burnout, Malicious Compliance, Week Survived (graded).

Crunch (Time = 0) raises Sanity costs 50%. Ally (Micah) grants one free lock per week. PTO skips a card for -3 Rep.
