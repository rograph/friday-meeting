EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK (ABRIDGED)

Sanity is your health. At zero, you burn out and the run ends. Emotional overhead is billed at cost: everything hurts one and a half times as much as the button implies, before the building settles further on top.
Time resets each morning. Run out and you enter Crunch: sanity costs rise 50%.
Credit is what your work is worth. Every result you produce appears on the At Risk tracker, shown in full at the end of each day.
Paper Trails lock an item so it cannot be claimed. Locks cost Time. Targets freeze at midnight Thursday.
Friday, 4:00 PM: someone reads the week aloud and claims your most valuable unlocked item, usually AVP Miller, sometimes one of his directors covering for him. You won't know who until the slide goes up. Later weeks, they claim more. Lock everything and nobody claims anything.
The Agenda: before the slide, the meeting runs items of business against the host's Composure, which you read the way the room does: a line above the card says how they're holding up, up to a cap of one item in week 1, two from week 2, three from week 7. Each item is a shot at you, in the host's own dialect. Deflecting is safe, costs Sanity (more every week), and lets Composure recover a little. Countering risks Sanity and Reputation on a roll (Reputation helps, later weeks hurt, Miller is the hardest to beat, your opening stance tilts the odds for the whole meeting), but a win does real damage, and a loss lets them recover more than a deflect would. Break them completely and Friday can claim nothing at all; wear it down partway and one fewer item gets claimed. The fight can end early if you break it, or run the full cap if you don't. There is no way to skip the agenda.
Development: secure every item two weeks running and the building adapts: that Friday you present the wins yourself, from slides you did not write. Whose framing survives decides what a clean week pays and how hungry the next one arrives. Paper Trails themselves are never broken. The building has other doors.
Notes: on Saturdays you may leave one line for Monday-you. Internal correspondence is reviewed. What arrives is usually what you wrote.
Reputation matters at the edges, and it accrues at a recalibrated rate: gains land at 70% of face value, losses land in full. The building keeps the difference. Below 30, you may not decline evening "opportunities." At 70 or above, your standing shields your single best unlocked item from being claimed (he cannot publicly take the headline win of someone that visible). At 0, you're performance managed out, a run-ending outcome, same as Burnout.
PTO skips a card. Its use is noted.
Termination (Burnout, or Performance Managed Out at 0 Reputation) ends more than the week: you restart at week 1. Upgrades and commendations survive; the Escape Plan does not survive unemployment. The building rehires. The building always rehires.
Leverage is paid at week's end and spent on Saturdays, on the version of you that leaves. Three purchases make up the Escape Plan. Complete it.
Leaving is the true ending, and it comes with an offer letter: a new position, a new cycle, also Everwell. Upgrades and commendations carry. The Escape Plan does not; it was the ticket. Each cycle the building settles harder, the agenda steadies itself when pressed, and the way out costs more than it did. The building is confident you will be back. The building has data.

The clock at the top is real. It starts the moment you clock in and runs the whole week, in actual seconds. Nothing in the building stops for it, and it does not stop for you.

This handbook is provided for your convenience. It is not a contract. Nothing is a contract.
